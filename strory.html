White Paper: Global Higher Educational Organization in Bogotá, Colombia

Background

Lake Forest Graduate School of Management provides Global Focus trips for students to gain and enrich their global mindset and global competencies. The trips are designed to expose students to global business including economics, cultural context, competitive landscape and global marketplace. In February of 2015, a group of students traveled to Bogotá, Colombia.

These students were required to partner with local multi-national businesses such as Grainger Colombia. The purpose was to analyze a current issue facing the company, work with local resources to develop a solution and finally present the solution with a full project plan that could be implemented upon approval. One of the graduate student groups had a focus on establishing Social Entrepreneurship that led to the findings in this white paper.

Value of the Experience

The trip has allowed our Lake Forest Graduate School of Management (LFGSM) team to gain a better perspective of the culture, business environment, political stability, and investment potential of Colombia. We have acquired invaluable skills on working with culturally sensitive issues, the process for developing business relationships, the importance of connectivity, and the right communication approach.

Prior to starting any social entrepreneurship in Colombia, it is absolutely crucial that the “right” people get the chance to meet us and understand our true mission. We feel fortunate to have made the right ecosystem connections in the non-profit, government and private investment sector. Part of the value of the experience included gaining a better understanding for the Colombian government’s priorities for shared prosperity and social equality. Our experience has allowed us to craft a unique social entrepreneurship program that will work hand in hand with our ecosystem of partners to assure a positive result. This exploratory trip has given us some valuable insights:

  • Social contributions impact the shared prosperity of Colombia
  • Need to respond immediately, to take advantage of the pro-tax environment
  • Prioritizing joint partnerships with the Colombian investment community can foster immediate opportunities

Post-trip, we have a better understanding for how our team and LFGSM can make an impact on the lives of young people in Colombia.

Why Colombia?

This democratic republic has strategic plans in place to address potential risk; facing Colombia, such as the current peace talks to minimize regional violence; they are investing $26Bn over the next four years to improve the country’s infrastructure. We made reference earlier to the business friendly environment, which includes non-tariff barriers to trade. There are currently over 200 US companies doing business in Colombia. These businesses are taking advantage of the pro-US environment, including: duty free import of raw materials, no VAT, Free Trade Zones near the airport and in key regional outposts, and 15% income tax versus the 34% in other Latin American markets.

The younger population of Colombia is thriving. They display tremendous passion and energy as they take part in the country’s revival. Our meetings informed us that this younger population is leading the way for the growing middle class, which will reach 46% of the population by 2025. This segment of the population provides Colombia with a solid base built on the following key qualities:

  • Skilled workforce
  • Disposable income
  • Growing educational base with solid literacy rates
  • Pro-Peace

We feels that the current positive economic trends have created a platform that allow NGO to be successful with the support of foundations, philanthropists, local government and social investors. With the support of our local partners were dedicated to giving more deserving students visibility to global business opportunities.

Importance of Social Entrepreneurship in Colombia

Despite the growing GDP, Colombia still relies heavily on commoditized products for much of their growth. There can be little productivity growth if they lack the capacity to innovate or from a competitive and trade context offer few incentives to innovate. "Supply of skills and knowledge in Colombia requires increased quality and relevance. Colombia is not producing students with enough 21st Century skills." Studies clearly indicate technical education on this process offers minimal impact. Tertiary education has failed to retain the best students and produce adequate results. Data suggests that the high level of inequality in the educational system directly correlates to the extreme poverty rates. World Bank data suggests that as of 2013, over 10% of the country’s population was in extreme poverty and another 35% was considered poor.

At the start of this year the Colombian government reacted, to these glaring statistics, by awarding close to 10,000 scholarships to talented students coming from the extreme poverty areas. While the scholarship program provides great benefit to these promising students, there are unaccounted social and financial hurdles these students must overcome in order to succeed. Therefore we can make a difference as to whether students from rural poverty areas of Colombia graduate or are sent back to their communities without acquiring the skills needed to make a true difference. We will do this by providing an infrastructure that augments the existing government scholarship program.

Great Opportunity and Why We will be Successful

Colombian President Juan Manual Santos promised during his 2014 inauguration speech that his top two priorities were ending the peace negotiations successfully and reducing poverty through improved education. His ultimate goal is to make Colombians the most educated country of Latin America. So why is higher education the key to moving more Colombians from poverty to middle class:

  • Only 37.2% of young Colombians continued their studies post high school
  • A Colombian with a bachelor’s degree earns about 3.5 times more than one with only a high school certificate
  • Around 17% of young Colombians remain unemployed
  • 39% of young Colombians never continued studies post secondary school
  • A Colombian with a Masters degree earns 8 times more

Hence, the current administration created the 2015 rollout of the new government scholarship program. The aim of the scholarship is to offer young people from poor backgrounds the opportunity to succeed in their higher education. Program specifics include:

  • In 2015, there were 9,676 students awarded scholarships
  • Currently there is $65M in USD allocated for the program in 2015
  • The Colombian government goal is to offer 40,000 scholarships by 2018
  • To qualify, students must be from poor backgrounds and attain high scores on the national exam. The scholarship is worth $142,863 in USD per student
  • The catch: if the student drops out, the tuition defaults to a government loan with interest

The tuition default is even more serious when you consider the ramifications on a family that is already operating at the extreme poverty level. These new student are entering a very challenging environment, where even some of their peers (who come from better economic backgrounds) are currently not succeeding in completing their higher educations. Along with the limitations of the current educational infrastructure we foresee there being additional social and financial hurdles such as:

  • Cost of living
  • Bullying
  • Safety
  • Temptations
  • Technology
  • Psychological stress
  • English language

The Solution

Our solution will provide the infrastructure to assure that these students will complete their degrees and become a positive force of change in their respective communities. More importantly in coordination with our partners in Colombia our value proposition will provide three core elements meant to produce different results: English Tutoring, Business Mentoring and Global Corporate Internships.

The GHEO program will use higher education to produce more global corporate ready talent that will drive more young Colombians to middle class and create more mentors for generations that follow. Our recommendation will be evaluated in a pilot program, which includes providing services, programs and funding for seven Political Science students in the scholarship program at Pontificia Universidad Javeriana in Bogotá. Our current estimate of total cost for the program is still being evaluated. But the support will go directly to the students for textbooks, transportation, and meals The fundraising efforts will be generated around US corporations that have a vested stake in Colombia.

The pilot program is strategically focused on one university and one department. It will be measured on a semester and yearly basis leading up to the student’s graduation in 2019. We are targeting the program launch for 2016, giving us ample time to handle all the logistics. Finally, our program will be closely linked to the government priorities of the Ministry of Education.

Our recommendations

  • Leverage the LFGSM faculty as a resource. That the faculty would be open to entertaining an educational program with Pontificia Universidad Javeriana to further enhance English/communication skills
  • Launch of our GHEO Value offering campaign, targeting US Corporations currently doing business in Colombia. "Corporate Social Responsibility is essential to the private sector’s continued longevity and prosperity. Such programs provide an opportunity for the private sector to show its commitment and responsibility to the communities and people that sustain them"
  • Coordinate with the Colombian Ministry of Education to drive higher education rates
  • Leverage multiple Colombian partnerships with organizations that provide infrastructure and resource capabilities
